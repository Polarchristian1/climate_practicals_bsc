Averages and anomalies#

A very common way to decompose time-series is to normalize them. Let \(A\) be any field, function of time \(t\). Then:

\[A(t) = \overline{A} + A'(t)\]

Where \(A'\) is called the fluctuating component of \(A\), \(A'(t)\) being the anomaly of \(A\) at the time \(t\). Often the variable t is omitted from the notation, i.e. one writes \(A = \overline{A} + A'\)

Zonal anomalies#

Similarly to the decomposition in time, geophysical fields can also be decomposed zonally:

\(A = \left[ A \right] + A^{*}\)

Where \(\left[ A \right]\) is the zonal average and \(A^{*}\) the departure from the zonal average.

Let’s compute \(\overline{T^{*}}\), which is the zonal anomaly of the temporal mean of the temperature field:

t_avg_dep = t2_tavg - t2_tavg.mean(dim='longitude')
